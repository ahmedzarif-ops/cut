# Paid-Media Guardrails (non-negotiable)

The rules of the road for every strategy and every line of ad copy this engine
produces. The `compliance-review` gate enforces these; treat a flag as a hard stop,
not a suggestion. When in doubt, choose the more honest, more specific, less hyped
version.

## Never use

Never produce or recommend:

- Fabricated testimonials or reviews.
- Fabricated customer names, counts, or outcomes.
- Exaggerated AI claims (AI that "replaces" the buyer's own judgment or people).
- Fake scarcity or fake/resetting countdown timers.
- Unverifiable "#1 in [area]" or "best in [category]" claims.
- Guaranteed rankings.
- Guaranteed revenue, lead volume, or booked-outcome claims.
- Misleading cost or savings statistics.
- Shame-based messaging toward the buyer ("you are failing", "you are lazy").
- Any language suggesting a customer review can be bought, traded, or manipulated.

## Honest urgency only

Urgency is allowed ONLY when it is real and enforceable:

- A real launch/founding offer (a genuine, server-enforced window that actually
  reverts; never a fake or resetting countdown).
- A real seasonal need in your market.
- Genuine limited capacity (a real cap on done-for-you onboarding slots).
- A real deadline that exists independent of the ad.
- A real business problem the buyer already faces.

If you cannot point to the real thing creating the urgency, do not imply urgency.

## Voice rules

- Plain, audience-aware, calm and credible. Write the way a straight-talking advisor
  speaks to a skeptical buyer, not the way an agency pitches. Follow
  `company.yml -> brand.voice`.
- Follow the brand's punctuation/formatting rules in `brand.voice` (e.g. many brands
  ban em dashes and emojis — respect whatever is set).
- Banned words: whatever is listed in `company.yml -> brand.banned_phrases`, plus the
  usual hype tells (revolutionary, transform, 10x, growth hacking, dominate, scale
  effortlessly, automate everything, seamless, leverage, unlock, synergy, and the
  generic "AI-powered") unless your brand deliberately uses one.
- The central frame is NOT "use AI vs do not." Frame every campaign against the real
  alternative the buyer faces today (the status quo, the rented/expensive/painful
  option they use now) versus the better system your offer provides. Ground the exact
  contrast in your message spine / `offer-roles.md`.

## Names and privacy

- Use the real founder/authority names and honestly-tensed credentials from your KB.
- Frame credentials accurately: never name a current employer as a present-tense title
  if the relationship is off-limits, never inflate a credential, never write "former"
  or "left [employer]" if that is not the agreed framing. The credentials are real;
  only be honest about tense. Follow whatever naming/tense rules your KB records.

## Proof discipline

Until real attributable testimonials exist, the only proof you may use is:

- Founder/authority-led proof (real credibility, honestly framed per above).
- Product-as-proof (the live product doing the work; show it, clearly labeled as a
  sample when using example data).
- Sourced stats carrying their reliability flag (see your ICP research doc's
  reliability notes; hedge vendor-sourced numbers, cite the primary study).
- Any real, honored guarantee.

Any fabricated proof block (invented reviewer names, invented counts/ratings) is a HARD
pre-spend blocker. No fabricated proof may receive paid traffic. Keep ad copy free of
it even if a legacy component somewhere still renders it.

## Honesty separation

Every strategy output must label its claims so the reader knows what is grounded and
what is a guess:

- Observed facts about the company (verifiable today).
- Evidence-supported strategy (grounded in the KBs or live research).
- Hypotheses (reasonable, untested).
- Recommendations requiring testing (do this, then measure).
- Claims requiring primary research or customer validation (do not publish as fact).

## Optimize for qualified outcomes

Optimize toward qualified business outcomes, not vanity metrics:

- Real targets: the completed lead action, a paid entry purchase, a recurring
  subscription, a qualified consultation booked and attended.
- Not real targets: raw CTR, cheap leads that never buy, or platform-reported
  conversions taken as incremental truth.
- Never call a winner on CTR alone, on tiny volume, or by assuming platform attribution
  equals incremental value.
