# The best bakery in the neighborhood is rarely the one people order from first

A customer who needs a cake for Saturday does not order from the most skilled
baker. They order from the one they can find and trust in ninety seconds. That is
usually the shop with forty recent reviews and an order button above the fold, not
the one with the best crumb.

Here is the specific fix for this week: ask your last ten in-store customers for a
review by text the same day they pick up their order. A same-day text gets a review
back about three times as often as an email two days later, because the visit is
still fresh and the phone is in their hand.

That one habit moves you up the local map results, which is where the Saturday-cake
orders come from.
