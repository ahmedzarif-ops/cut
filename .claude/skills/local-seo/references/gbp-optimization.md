# GBP Optimization — the full checklist

Open this when doing hands-on Google Business Profile work for a client, or auditing a suspension risk. The GBP is the listing that appears in the map pack; a complete, accurate, guideline-clean profile is the foundation of local ranking — the highest-leverage single asset you control. Sourcing note: GBP mechanics and guidelines below track Google's own docs — "Improve your Business Profile ranking on Google" and "Guidelines for representing your business on Google Business Profile" (support.google.com) — verify current wording before quoting to a client, Google changes these.

## Categories — the single biggest relevance lever

- **Primary category** = the most weight. Choose the **single most accurate** category, not the broadest. "Dentist" > "Doctor". "Italian restaurant" > "Restaurant". "Auto repair shop" > "Auto". Being specific wins the queries that convert.
- **Secondary categories** for genuinely offered secondary services only. Don't add categories for services not actually offered — dilutes relevance and risks a guideline flag.
- Audit the winners: whoever ranks in the client's map pack — check their primary category (visible via profile inspection tools). If the whole pack uses a category the client doesn't, that's often the fix.
- Re-check periodically — Google adds/renames categories.

## Services

- Fill out the **services list** under each category with the real jobs the client does. Add short honest descriptions.
- Match service names to how customers search. These support relevance for long-tail service queries.

## Service-area, address, hours

- **Service-area business (SAB)** — a business that serves customers at the customer's location. They **hide the street address** and set a **service area** (list the real towns/cities/ZIPs served, Google caps the count — pick the highest-value ones). Do NOT list a service area you don't truly serve.
- **Storefront** (a showroom, shop, or office with walk-ins) shows the address. Don't fake a storefront to get a pin in a town.
- **Hours** accurate; set **24 hours** / emergency hours if genuinely offered (big win for "emergency [service] near me"). Keep holiday hours current.

## Photos — steady cadence, real work

- Freshness and volume both help (trust + a mild activity signal). Upload on a **steady cadence** (e.g. a few per week), not one dump.
- Real content only: job sites, before/afters, storefront, team, equipment, completed work. Honest imagery. No stock photos passed off as the client's work.
- Cover: logo, cover photo, exterior/team, and a growing library of real jobs.

## Google Posts, Q&A, tiles

- **Google Posts** — publish updates/offers/seasonal tips ~weekly. Keeps the profile active and surfaces content in the panel. Honest offers only.
- **Q&A** — seed the common real questions (with honest answers) and **monitor** it; anyone can post an answer, so unmonitored Q&A is a misinformation and competitor-sabotage risk.
- **Products / services tiles** — use them to list offerings with photos + honest pricing ranges where sensible.
- **Messaging / booking** — enable if the client can actually respond fast (speed-to-lead matters); don't enable a channel that goes unanswered.

## Verification, duplicates, completeness

- **Verify** the listing (Google's process; can require video verification). Unverified profiles don't compete.
- **One listing per real location.** Hunt and **suppress duplicate listings** (common after moves, rebrands, or agency churn) — duplicates split prominence and can trigger a suspension.
- **Complete every field.** Completeness correlates with rank and conversion; empty fields leave relevance on the table.

## Guideline & spam safety — protect the listing

A suspended GBP removes the client's #1 channel instantly. Never do any of the following (all are Google guideline violations and/or suspension triggers):

- **Keyword-stuffed name.** The GBP name must be the **exact real-world business name** — no added keywords, cities, or taglines ("ABC Auto," not "ABC Auto | 24/7 Emergency Brake Repair Austin"). This is the most common self-inflicted suspension.
- **Fake / lead-gen / virtual-office / PO-box address**, or any address the business doesn't legitimately operate from. No renting an address to fake proximity into a town.
- **Fake locations or duplicate listings** to blanket a metro. A SAB gets ONE listing at its real location + a service area — not a pin in every suburb.
- **Fake, bought, or incentivized-without-disclosure reviews; review-gating** (see `citations-and-reviews.md`) — a Google policy violation and illegal under the US FTC rule.
- **Misrepresenting hours, categories, or services.**

### If a listing is already violating

Fixing it honestly is part of the engagement. Correct the name/address/categories to compliant real-world values. If already **suspended**, file reinstatement through Google's process with evidence the business is real and now compliant (business license, real photos, proof of address/service). Set the client's expectation: recovery can take weeks and isn't guaranteed — which is exactly why we never risk it in the first place.

## Fast GBP audit checklist

- [ ] Verified, single listing, no duplicates
- [ ] Primary category = most specific accurate one; secondaries real
- [ ] Services filled + described; match search language
- [ ] SAB address hidden / storefront address correct; real service area
- [ ] Hours accurate (+ emergency/24h if true)
- [ ] Real photos, growing on a cadence
- [ ] Google Posts active; Q&A seeded + monitored; tiles used
- [ ] Name = real-world name, zero keyword stuffing
- [ ] No fake/lead-gen address; no fake pins
- [ ] Reviews honest, responded to, steady velocity (see citations-and-reviews.md)
