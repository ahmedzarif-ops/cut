# Blog Brand Voice (canonical)

The single source of truth for how your company's blog sounds. Apply to every post.
This is a living document: the blog agents may PROPOSE refinements (flag for human review),
but never silently rewrite it.

**Seed this file once, from config, then refine over runs.** Pull the starting rules from
`company.yml` → `brand.voice`, `brand.banned_phrases`, `brand.visual_notes`, and ground the
audience section in `company.yml` → `icp.description` and the full `icp.research_doc`. The
headings below are the structure to fill; the bracketed prompts show what belongs in each.
Do not invent audience or offer facts here — copy them from config/research.

## Tone
[The voice in one or two sentences. Confident without bravado, respectful of the reader's

## Hard rules (never break)
- Apply every rule in `company.yml` → `brand.voice` and `brand.banned_phrases`.
- No hype, no superlatives you cannot back up, no fake urgency, no "guaranteed", no "#1".
- No fabricated stats, testimonials, or case studies. Cite real, named sources. If you
  cannot source a claim, cut it.
- Plain words over jargon. If a term needs explaining, explain it in one clause.
- (Example house rule some brands add: NO em dashes — use commas, periods, or "and"; write
  ranges as "15 to 85" and money as "12,000 dollars"; NO emojis. Keep only the punctuation
  rules your brand actually mandates.)

## Sentence and structure patterns
- Second person ("you"), present tense, short-to-medium sentences.
- Open by naming the reader's exact pain in their own words (validate, do not lecture).
- Then reframe: the comforting-but-wrong belief, replaced with the freeing truth.
- Teach generously with scannable lists and an ordered "start here" plan.
- Be honest about nuance (state the caveat). Honesty is a trust signal and EEAT gold.
- Earn the offer mention: only after real value, framed as "this is the gap we close."
- Close with a calm bottom line and one low-pressure CTA.
