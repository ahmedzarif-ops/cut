# Segmentation

Who we target and how the message shifts. Segment by these five dimensions. Ground audience picks
here; the personas come from your ICP KB (`company.yml` → `icp.research_doc`). A subscriber
carries a value on each dimension; the strategist uses the combination to pick the program, the
angle, and the cadence.

## 1. Funnel stage (the spine of every flow)

List the funnel stages that match YOUR offer ladder (from `offer.summary`). The generic shape,
top to bottom — adapt the rung names to your real offer:

1. Newsletter-only subscriber
2. Content/blog subscriber
3. Free-thing visitor (landing page for your free diagnostic/audit/trial)
4. Free-thing starter
5. Free-thing abandoner
6. Free-thing completer
7. Low-score / high-urgency completer
8. Entry-offer page visitor
9. Entry-offer checkout abandoner
10. Entry-offer buyer
11. Entry-offer buyer who has not started
12. Entry-offer buyer who started but has not completed
13. Entry-offer completer
14. Buyer eligible for the core offer
15. Core-offer page visitor
16. Core-offer checkout abandoner
17. Core-offer subscriber
18. Subscriber who has not activated key features
19. Active subscriber
20. At-risk or canceled subscriber
21. Premium-tier prospect
22. Consultation booked / no-show / attended / unclosed
23. Customer eligible for review, referral, testimonial, or case study

## 2. Segment (vertical / type)

The buckets your ICP splits into (from `icp.research_doc`) — industry, use case, or customer type.
Map each segment to its pain, its best-timed trigger, and its best angle. Keep a small matrix so a
subscriber's segment picks the pain and the angle automatically.

## 3. Buyer reality

The situational buckets that change the message: urgent/reactive vs. considered/high-ticket buyer;
solo vs. team vs. multi-unit; DIY-minded vs. done-for-you; vendor-frustrated; dependent on a
channel they want to escape; technology-curious vs. technology-skeptical; early-stage vs.
growth-stage.

- Vendor-frustrated → lead the alternative contrast and any real guarantee.
- Channel-dependent → lead "stop renting X / own the Y" (your central contrast).
- Technology-skeptical → lead the outcome and product-as-proof ("nothing happens without your
  approval"); do not lead with the technology.
- Technology-curious → the demo and the system framing convert fast.

## 4. Primary weak factor (the email's center of gravity)

The single factor (from `message-factor-model.md`) where this subscriber is weakest. For a
diagnostic completer, the primary weak factor branches the Results Nurture sequence (program D):
each branch explains why THAT factor costs them and gives one practical action.

## 5. Engagement

Highly engaged, moderately engaged, low engagement, unengaged, re-engaged, frequency-reduced,
unsubscribed/suppressed.

- Engagement governs cadence and the global frequency cap. High-engagement + a started action
  earns more frequency; low/unengaged triggers the re-engagement program (K), then frequency
  reduction, then sunset.

## Personas

Pull the real personas — names, revenue/size bands, awareness stage, objections, and the
anti-persona (who NOT to chase first) — from `company.yml` → `icp.research_doc`. If no persona
research exists yet, run ICP research first; never invent personas.
