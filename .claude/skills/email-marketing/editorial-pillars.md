# Ongoing Email Content Pillars

The editorial system for the newsletter and content flows. Every recurring email maps to one
pillar; the pillars are your message spine's pillars, so email reinforces the same story as
every other surface.

## Derive your pillars from the spine + the offer

Build 5-7 recurring content pillars once per company:
- The FIRST pillars are the pillars of your message spine (`kb_dir`) — so recurring email is
  on-message by construction.
- The NEXT pillars cover the recurring objections and adoption realities your offer must resolve
  (from `icp.research_doc` and `offer.summary`): the central contrast your brand draws, the "how
  this fits a busy buyer's week" angle, and the top objection your offer resolves.

Each recurring email is filed under exactly one pillar. This is the method; the specific pillar
names are yours.

### Example pillar set (illustrative — replace with your own)

1. **<Spine pillar 1>** — the topics under your first spine pillar.
2. **<Spine pillar 2>** — the topics under your second spine pillar.
3. **<Spine pillar 3>** — the topics under your third spine pillar.
4. **<Your central contrast>** — the "do this instead of that" reframe your brand is built on.
5. **<Fits-their-week>** — small, high-leverage actions; the operating rhythm; what to delegate.
6. **<Resolve the top objection>** — the recurring objection your offer answers (control, risk,
   complexity, trust), resolved honestly.

## Editorial-calendar framework

Plan recurring sends by rotating the pillars against the season and the active segments. A
practical cadence: one value-first newsletter per week, pillar rotation across the month, and
honest seasonal inserts (only where a real seasonal need exists for your audience) as timing
levers. The strategist produces the framework + the ranked first-20; a dated calendar instance
is a later, separate output.
