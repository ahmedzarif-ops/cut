# Value-First Operating Principles

The operating discipline that makes this an email system worth subscribing to, not a
promotional blast. Every email obeys these.

## One useful lesson per email

Each email focuses on ONE problem, insight, practical fix, template, checklist, diagnostic
interpretation, implementation example, or next action. Do not cram unrelated lessons into
one message. If you have two lessons, you have two emails.

## Teach before asking (the conversion sequence)

For a conversion email, use this order:
1. Identify a costly but recognizable issue.
2. Explain why it matters in the reader's own terms (their outcomes, their numbers, their work).
3. Give a useful action the reader can take now.
4. Explain the limitation of solving that issue in isolation.
5. Offer the next step ONLY after the value exchange.

Do not disguise a sales pitch as education. The value must stand on its own even if the
reader never clicks the CTA.

## Make value tangible

Build emails around real assets the reader can use in under 15 minutes. Derive the specific
assets from your own offer and ICP (`company.yml` → `offer.summary`, `icp.research_doc`). The
shape is what matters: a short audit, a ready-to-use template, a checklist, a script, a
worksheet, a diagnostic they can self-run. Examples of the FORM (adapt the nouns to your
domain):
- A 5-minute self-audit of the one thing that most costs the reader results
- A fill-in-the-blank template (an outreach message, a follow-up, a response)
- A checklist for a task they keep getting wrong
- A worksheet that reframes a decision (e.g. cost-of-X vs cost-of-Y)
- A short script for a conversation they avoid

Give the exact words ("Here is the exact message you can use") rather than telling them to
write their own.

## Earn the next email

- Behavioral flows may send more frequently when the user explicitly requested an asset or
  started an action. Newsletter emails must stay useful even if the recipient never buys.
- Use a global frequency cap across overlapping automations (a subscriber in three flows
  does not get three emails a day).
- Suppress irrelevant promotions for current customers.
- Offer frequency preferences and a clear, one-click unsubscribe path.
- Reduce or sunset email to persistently disengaged subscribers (see program K and
  `data-and-deliverability.md`).

## Personalize only when it helps

Useful fields: first name, company/business name, segment, city/region, primary interest,
account stage, lead source, diagnostic score, primary weak factor, content interest, product
progress, activation status, existing tools, current challenge.

Never fabricate data, imply a live audit that did not run, or use personalization in a way
that feels invasive. A segment or weak-factor token that sharpens relevance is good; a
"we noticed you were looking at..." that the data does not support is not.
