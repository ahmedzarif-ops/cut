# Lifecycle Programs (A-L)

The required programs. For each, the strategist defines the full spec shape; the copywriter writes
the ready-to-send emails. Every program spec must define: entry trigger, eligibility, exclusions,
exit criteria, re-entry rules, wait times, branch logic, primary CTA, frequency-cap interaction,
owner-handoff conditions, and measurement plan.

Map every offer reference below onto YOUR real ladder (`company.yml` → `offer.summary`,
`offer.pricing_notes`). "The free thing / the entry offer / the core offer / the premium tier" are
the generic rungs from `offer-roles.md`.

The FOUNDATION run builds A, D, F, G first (the core revenue path). The rest follow per the ranked
first-20 + the 90-day roadmap.

## A. Newsletter Welcome Sequence (FOUNDATION)
- Audience: new newsletter subscribers. Objective: build trust and make the subscriber more
  capable before asking them to buy.
- 5-7 emails: (1) welcome + core insight (the one belief your brand converts people to); (2) quick
  self-audit (the places their results leak); (3) practical fix (a top-factor quick win); (4)
  practical fix (proof / credibility); (5) practical fix (responsiveness / follow-up); (6) your
  central contrast (own the asset vs. rent it); (7) soft invite to the free thing.
- Rule: the first CTA is NOT a purchase; every email carries a usable action; segment and
  engagement branches where useful.

## B. Content-Upgrade / Blog Subscriber Sequence
- Audience: subscribers from a blog post or a content asset. Branches by the topic that brought
  them in.
- Structure: (1) deliver/extend the exact topic; (2) deepen with a concrete fix; (3) connect to
  the factor model; (4) invite the free thing to find their highest priority.

## C. Free-Thing Start / Abandonment
- Audience: users who start but do not complete the free diagnostic/audit/trial. 3-4 emails,
  helpful not guilt-based. Themes: it is quick; it surfaces a priority not generic advice; you do
  not need to be technical; you likely got pulled away; you just need to see what to fix first.
  CTA: continue the free thing.

## D. Free-Thing Completion / Results Nurture (FOUNDATION)
- Audience: completers who have not bought the entry offer. 7-10 emails, BRANCHED by primary weak
  factor (from `message-factor-model.md`).
- Each branch: why that factor costs them; one practical action per email; a checklist / script /
  example where possible; why scattered fixes do not make a working system; position the entry
  offer as the way to turn results into a prioritized, specific plan; note any credit toward the
  core offer; verified proof only; a low-pressure free-preview path when active; close with choice
  architecture (DIY entry offer / core offer / consultation).

## E. Entry-Offer Product-Interest / Checkout Abandonment
- Audience: entry-offer page visitors or checkout starters who do not buy. 3-5 emails resolving:
  will it work for my situation; is it generic; do I need to be technical; can I do it in one
  sitting; what will I finish with; is this another thing I will not complete; why pay when free
  advice exists; what if I later need ongoing help; does the entry price credit toward the core
  offer; preview and refund policies. Do not lead with discounts.

## F. Entry-Offer Buyer Onboarding and Activation (FOUNDATION)
- Audience: new entry-offer buyers. A 10-14 day flow to drive start, progress, completion, and
  quick wins: (1) confirmation + next step; (2) start here; (3) make time (do it in one sitting);
  (4) quick win (fix the top gap); (5) common blocker + solution; (6) build proof; (7) improve a
  weak factor; (8) progress check; (9) completion prompt; (10) next decision (run it yourself /
  delegate / the core offer).
- Personalize by segment, diagnostic result, progress, incomplete step, engagement.

## G. Entry-Offer to Core-Offer Upgrade (FOUNDATION)
- Audience: active/completed entry-offer customers where execution is the bottleneck. Core reframe:
  "The plan is not the hard part. Staying consistent while you run the business is."
- 6-8 emails: the execution gap; what the system does; approval-first / owner control intact; work
  in the owner's voice; the recurring workflows; the priorities dashboard; tool/CRM connection;
  any credit toward month one if current; cancel-anytime if current; when the core offer is NOT the
  right fit; consultation option for complex situations.

## H. Core-Offer Product-Interest / Demo / Checkout Abandonment
- Audience: product-page visitors, demo requesters, checkout abandoners, high-engagement prospects.
  6-8 emails resolving: will it sound generic; will it act without approval; setup; do I need a new
  tool/CRM; can I use it from my phone; will it work for my situation; what does it do each week;
  why better than a vendor/agency; can I cancel; how will I know it is working; should I use the
  entry offer instead; when do I need the premium tier. CTAs: subscribe / start with the entry
  offer / request a live sample / book a consultation.

## I. Core-Offer Onboarding, Activation, Retention, and Risk
- Audience: new and active core-offer subscribers. A 30/60/90-day system: confirmation + first-day
  setup; voice/config/guardrail setup; tool/CRM connect + data import; first approval request; first
  value delivered; the recurring workflows activated; weekly digest; monthly progress report;
  feature-adoption prompts; stalled-user intervention; at-risk rescue; feedback loop; referral
  invite only after real value. Activation milestones: diagnostic done; profile configured; voice
  configured; first item approved; first value action; first workflow active; first measurable
  improvement reviewed; first 30-day rhythm completed.

## J. Premium-Tier / Consultation
- Audience: larger/complex/high-intent buyers. Booking confirmation; pre-call questionnaire + prep;
  reminder; no-show recovery; post-call recap; personalized recommendation; proposal follow-up;
  "not now" value nurture; closed-lost reactivation. Distinguish when a business should use the
  entry offer, the core offer, the premium tier, or none.

## K. Re-Engagement and Preference Center
- Audience: low-engagement and inactive subscribers. Relevance-reset email; topic-preference
  survey; segment/stage update; frequency-reduction option; "choose your next guide"; final
  re-permission; suppression and sunset logic.

## L. Customer Advocacy, Referrals, and Case Studies
- Audience: customers who hit a real milestone. Flows for product reviews, testimonials, case
  studies, referrals, advisory participation, feature feedback, founder interviews. Trigger only
  after real usage/success signals. Never propose incentives that violate review-platform policy.
  Asks stay transparent and low-pressure. These flows COLLECT real proof; the emails do not
  fabricate it.

## Progress-based product/course lifecycle (optional, when applicable)
If your offer includes a self-paced product or course, run a PROGRESS-based (NEVER time-based)
lifecycle: welcome → setup-success → first-win → stall-recovery → completion → gap → invite. Each
step fires on a real learner PROGRESS signal (a setup check passing, a checkpoint completed), never
on a clock/elapsed-time trigger. Recovery is keyed to the SPECIFIC step the learner stalled on, in
the value-first voice — help them past the real blocker; never a generic "you forgot to finish."
