# The Message-Factor Model (map every email to your shared story)

Every email, workflow, recommendation, and CTA should map to at least one FACTOR in your
company's value model, and each factor maps to one PILLAR of your message spine — so email tells
the same story as your website, your decks, and every other surface.

## Build your factor/pillar model from the message spine

Your factors and pillars are NOT generic — derive them from your message spine (`kb_dir`) and
your offer (`company.yml` → `offer.summary`). The METHOD:

1. Take the 2-4 PILLARS of your message spine (the top-level story your brand tells).
2. Under each pillar, list the concrete FACTORS a buyer is actually evaluated on — the specific,
   nameable things that determine whether they get the outcome your offer promises.
3. Every email maps to one factor (and therefore one pillar). This is what keeps email on-message.

### Example (illustrative only — replace with your own)

A services company whose spine is "Get found → Win the job → Keep the customer" might use factors
like: Findability and Proof (under Get found); Clarity and Trust (bridging into Win the job);
Responsiveness (Win the job); Follow-up (Keep the customer). A SaaS company's factors would be
entirely different (e.g. Activation, Time-to-value, Habit, Expansion under a "adopt → rely →
expand" spine). Do not copy the services example — build the model your spine implies.

## Per-recommendation fields (state these for every email recommendation)

For each email the strategist or copywriter proposes, state:

- Factor category (which factor from your model).
- Pillar (which pillar of the message spine).
- Audience segment (from `segmentation.md`).
- Intended business outcome (the qualified outcome, not an open or click).
- Email type: education, activation, conversion, retention, or reactivation.
- Primary CTA (one).
- Why that CTA logically follows the value delivered.

This keeps every email earning its CTA and mapping to the one story the whole company tells.
