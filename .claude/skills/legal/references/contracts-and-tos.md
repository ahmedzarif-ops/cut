## The documents the company likely needs

- **Terms of Service** (the customer agreement governing use of the site/product) and **Privacy Policy**
  (what's collected/used/shared + consumer rights — see `privacy-and-data.md`). Both must be **truthful to
  actual practice**; a policy that misstates practice is itself an FTC deception risk.
- **Subscription/billing terms** — if the offer includes recurring billing, auto-renewal law applies (below).
- **MSAs / SOWs** for higher-touch or service engagements.
- **Independent-contractor / contributor agreements** (incl. IP assignment — see `corporate-and-ip.md`).
- **DPAs** with vendors/processors that touch personal data.

## Auto-renewal / subscription law (recurring billing)

If the company bills recurring subscriptions, this is live:
- **Federal: ROSCA** (Restore Online Shoppers' Confidence Act) requires clear & conspicuous disclosure of
  terms, **express informed consent** before charging, and a **simple cancellation** mechanism.
- The FTC's **"Click-to-Cancel" Negative Option Rule was VACATED by the Eighth Circuit on July 8, 2025** (a
  procedural ruling) — so that specific federal rule is **not in force**, but ROSCA and **state auto-renewal
  laws still apply**, and the FTC/state AGs continue enforcing against deceptive subscription practices. The
  FTC opened new negative-option rulemaking (ANPRM) in Jan 2026.
- **State auto-renewal laws** remain in force and stricter in places — e.g. **California's Automatic Renewal
  Law (amended effective July 1, 2025)** requires consent to the auto-renewal terms and tighter cancellation.
Practical posture: clear renewal terms, affirmative consent at signup, easy self-serve cancel, renewal
reminders where required. Confirm specifics with counsel.
Sources: [WilmerHale: Eighth Circuit vacates Click-to-Cancel](https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/insights/client-alerts/20250801-eighth-circuit-vacates-the-ftcs-click-to-cancel-rule-but-federal-and-state-regulators-likely-to-remain-active) · [FTC Negative Option / ROSCA](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/advertising-marketing) (verify current at runtime).

## Contract-review checklist (issue-spotting, not advice)

When reviewing any agreement, scan for:
- **Parties & signatures** — correct legal entities; authority to sign.
- **Scope / deliverables** — what's actually promised; SLAs.
- **Payment** — amount, schedule, late fees, refunds, **auto-renewal terms**.
- **Term & termination** — length, renewal, how to exit, notice periods.
- **Liability & indemnity** — caps, mutual vs one-sided, carve-outs (the highest-risk clauses → counsel).
- **IP ownership & license** — who owns deliverables/content; assignment vs license (see `corporate-and-ip.md`).
- **Confidentiality / NDA** terms.
- **Warranties & disclaimers**; **"as-is"** limits.
- **Governing law & dispute resolution** — venue, arbitration, class-action waiver.
- **Data terms** — DPA, security, breach notice where personal data is involved.
- **Red flags** — uncapped liability, broad indemnity you grant, automatic IP assignment away from the
  company, perpetual/irrevocable grants, unilateral change terms, auto-renewal with hard cancellation.

## Route to counsel when…

- **Signing or amending** any agreement with real consequence (always, for liability/indemnity/IP terms).
- Drafting/changing the **ToS, Privacy Policy, or billing terms**.
- A **counterparty dispute**, breach, or termination fight.
- Anything with **uncapped liability, broad indemnity, or IP assignment** implications.
