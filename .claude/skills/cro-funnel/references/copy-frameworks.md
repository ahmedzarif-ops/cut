# Copy Frameworks Library + Voice Rules

Select frameworks intentionally per section; do not apply all mechanically.

## Voice (enforced on every word)

The voice is: direct, plain, audience-aware, practical, respectful, honest, anti-jargon, non-hype,
founder-led where appropriate. Pull the exact tone rules and the banned list from `company.yml` →
`brand.voice` and `brand.banned_phrases`.

BANNED words and patterns (the page fails review if any appear): generic hype (revolutionary,
seamless, leverage, unlock, synergy, transform your business, 10x), broad unprovable guarantees,
generic template language, fake urgency, fear-mongering, and superiority claims that cannot be
proven — plus anything in `brand.banned_phrases`.

Language patterns that fit the brand: write them from your VoC grid (`voice-of-customer.template.md`)
and message-spine KB — the buyer's own words for the pain, the desired outcome, and the "you stay in
control" promise. Reuse and adapt those; never invent language the buyer would not use.

## Framework selection

- **PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solve)** → the opening of a problem-aware page. Name the pain, make the cost felt, then the fix. Use for the problem section.
- **BAB (Before, After, Bridge)** → paint the stronger future state, then your offer is the bridge. Good for the higher-tier mechanism story.
- **4Ps (Promise, Picture, Proof, Push)** → the spine of a full sales page. Promise the outcome, picture it, prove it (founder-led if pre-testimonial), push with the offer + guarantee.
- **QUEST (Qualify, Understand, Educate, Stimulate, Transition)** → higher-consideration / consult pages where you must qualify and educate before the ask.
- **Hook → Insight → Mechanism → Proof → CTA** → short-form page SECTIONS and ad-to-page message match.
- **Claim → So What → Business Consequence** → feature-to-benefit translation. Every feature becomes: the claim, why it matters to the buyer, the money/outcome consequence. Do this for each feature in your offer, grounded in the real value.

## Practical methodology (prioritized, not mechanical)

1. **Voice of Customer first (Wiebe / Copyhackers).** Write in the buyer's words from `voice-of-customer.template.md`; do not invent language.
2. **Message match (Gardner / Unbounce).** The page headline must match the ad that drove the click; one most-wanted action; high attention ratio.
3. **Awareness stage (Schwartz).** Open where the buyer is. Problem-aware cold traffic starts at the pain, not the product.
4. **Specific, reason-why, measurable (Hopkins / Caples / Ogilvy).** Concrete claims with a reason; research-led; specificity beats adjectives.
5. **Ethical persuasion (Cialdini).** Real authority (founder-led), genuine social proof (only when you have it), reciprocity (a genuine free step), genuine scarcity only (a real, enforced window/price).
6. **Conversion Sequence + LIFT (McGlaughlin / Goward).** Maximize motivation and value-prop clarity; minimize friction and anxiety; the guarantee and "cancel anytime" cut anxiety.
7. **Cognitive load + usability (Ash).** Short lines, scannable, mobile-first, one idea per block.
8. **Jobs to Be Done (Moesta).** Frame around the progress the buyer wants and the anxieties that block the switch.

## Feature-to-benefit blocks

Build one per feature in your offer using Claim → So What → Business Consequence, grounded in
`offer.summary` and the ICP research. Tag each with the six-factor it strengthens (see
`six-factor-model.md`). Never inflate the consequence beyond what the product actually delivers.
