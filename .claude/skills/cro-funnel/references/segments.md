# Segments (do not treat your audience as one)

A METHOD for segmenting the audience, grounded in your ICP research (`icp.research_doc`). Do not
treat the whole ICP as a single buyer — derive 2-4 real segments from the research and build page
messaging per segment. The Message Research Brief picks the segment; this file gives the angle
structure.

## For each segment, capture

- **Who they are** — the concrete sub-group (by use case, ticket size, urgency, or maturity), from research.
- **Buyer reality** — what is actually true about how they buy: urgency level, decision-cycle length, what matters most, and where they lose business today.
- **Best message angles** — 3-4 hooks that speak to that reality, in the buyer's own words (pull from the VoC grid).
- **Which pains hit hardest** — name the specific ICP pains that dominate for this segment.
- **Which six-factor levers lead** — of Findability / Proof / Clarity / Trust / Responsiveness / Follow-up (see `six-factor-model.md`), which 2-3 are the primary conversion levers for this segment.

## Illustrative shape (replace with YOUR research-derived segments)

- A **high-urgency / fast-response** segment — speed and visibility matter most; loss happens when the buyer is not found or does not respond quickly. Lead levers: Findability, Responsiveness.
- A **high-consideration / high-ticket** segment — longer cycle; trust, proof, and quote follow-up matter more than instant booking. Lead levers: Proof, Trust, Follow-up.
- A **growth-stage / multi-unit** segment — needs visibility, accountability, and a system more than another tactic; the natural path to a higher offer tier. Lead levers: Follow-up, Clarity.

These are shapes, not answers. Fill them from your ICP research; never inherit another company's segments.

## How to use per page

- A paid-social page targets ONE segment. Match the hook, the proof, the objections, and the offer emphasis to that segment.
- The low-ticket entry suits most segments but usually converts the immediate/concrete segments fastest.
- Higher-tier emphasis shifts by segment — align the primary factor lever above to the tier's promise.
