# The Six-Factor Conversion Model

Every page audit, wireframe, recommendation, and test hypothesis MUST evaluate these six factors,
and every recommendation MUST tag which factor(s) it improves. This is the shared vocabulary between
the brief, the page-blueprints, and the cro-scorecard. The factors are generic conversion levers;
translate each "page implication" to your own offer and buyer from config.

## 1. Findability

- Does the buyer discover the business where they actually look (search, listings, the channels your ICP uses)?
- Is the presence in those channels clear and complete?
- Page implication: for discovery-driven segments, lead with "show up where your customers look"; a live diagnostic that proves a findability gap is strong.

## 2. Proof

- Are relevant results, examples, reviews, and specific outcomes visible and credible?
- Does proof feel relevant to this exact buyer?
- Page implication: if pre-testimonial, proof on YOUR pages is founder-led + product-as-proof + sourced stats + guarantee (see `ethics-guardrails.md`). Never fabricate.

## 3. Clarity

- Can the buyer immediately understand what you do, who it is for, and what happens next?
- Is the offer described in the buyer's language, not abstract jargon?
- Page implication: one most-wanted action per page; plain language; no jargon.

## 4. Trust

- Does the page look credible, transparent, and grounded?
- Are pricing, cancellation, refund policy, process, and privacy clear?
- Page implication: show the real price, the guarantee, the "you stay in control" promise, and the founder credibility.

## 5. Responsiveness

- Does the page communicate the cost of a slow or missing response in the buyer's world?
- Does it explain how the offer protects leads/quotes from going cold?
- Page implication: use the relevant speed / lost-to-silence evidence (with reliability flags) for time-sensitive segments.

## 6. Follow-up

- Does the conversion experience naturally move users up the offer ladder?
- Are non-buyers nurtured based on what they learned in the diagnostic/entry step?
- Page implication: this is the funnel itself — the upsell path and the exit-intent downsell, plus the contact capture at the entry step.

## Usage rule

In the cro-scorecard and in every page recommendation, write the factor tag explicitly, for example:
"Adds the guarantee near the CTA → improves Trust." A page that cannot point to which factors it
strengthens is not finished.
