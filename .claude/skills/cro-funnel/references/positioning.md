# Positioning (the strategic center of every page)

Read this before writing any funnel copy. It is a METHOD for building your positioning, not a
fixed positioning — the specifics come from `company.yml` (`offer.summary`, `icp.description`) and
your ICP research (`icp.research_doc`). Never invent the facts below; derive them.

## Build these elements before you write copy

1. **The thesis (the spine of all messaging).** One sentence naming the shift you are selling — the
   difference between how the buyer operates today and the better state you move them to. Pull it
   from the core insight in your ICP research. Every page inherits this spine.
2. **What you are.** One practical sentence: what the buyer gets, in their language, and how they
   stay in control. Ground it in `offer.summary`.
3. **What you are NOT.** List the adjacent categories the buyer might mistake you for and explicitly
   disown each. Positioning against the wrong category is the most common conversion leak.
4. **Core themes.** A short list of repeatable message beats you pull from per page (the outcome,
   the pain removed, the control the buyer keeps). Keep them true to the ICP research.

## Competitive alternatives (April Dunford frame)

The buyer is not choosing between products in your category. They are choosing between the
alternatives they would otherwise use. List each real alternative from your ICP research and write
the one-line way your page quietly beats it. Every page should beat the alternative most relevant to
its segment. Typical alternative classes to fill in from research:

- The status-quo aggregator / rented channel the buyer resents paying for → you = own it instead of rent it.
- The expensive done-for-you service / retainer → you = the transparent, self-serve or lighter-touch version.
- DIY (a spreadsheet, ad hoc effort) → you = the system that runs consistently, the owner just approves.
- The heavy incumbent tool that is too expensive/complex for this buyer → you = the complementary layer they will actually use.

## Honest distinctions (keep them true)

From your ICP research, capture the true, defensible distinctions — where you are COMPLEMENTARY to a
tool the buyer already keeps (never claim to replace what you do not), the honest answer to "why not
just use a generic AI chat tool" (a chat box cannot see the buyer's real context, remember, or act),
and the specific under-served slice of the market that the big tools are not built for. Do not
overclaim; distinctions must survive scrutiny.

## Founder-led proof is core (see ethics-guardrails.md for the rules)

If the company is pre-testimonial, the founder pairing carries credibility. Present real credentials,
past-framed, from `owner.name` and any founder KB under `kb_dir`. Always apply the NAME and PRIVACY
rules in `ethics-guardrails.md`.
