# Voice-of-Customer Capture (the words that convert)

Pull from your ICP research (`company.yml` → `icp.research_doc`) FIRST — it should already hold raw
buyer voice from research (community scrapes, forum sources, interviews). Only gather more if the
brief reveals a gap. Use real buyer language; never invent attributed quotes.

## Capture grid (fill per page)

- **Pains** (in their words): the exact phrasing the buyer uses for what hurts.
- **Desired outcomes**: what they actually want (the progress, not the feature).
- **Trigger events** (what makes them look now): the moment that starts the search.
- **Failed alternatives** (what they tried and resented): the tools/services they abandoned.
- **Distrust signals** (what makes them bounce): jargon, hype, fake-looking testimonials, vague claims, no real price.
- **Perceived risks**: wasting money again, locking into a contract, too complex to adopt, looking foolish.
- **Exact customer phrases** (verbatim, safe to paraphrase for tone): pull from the "raw buyer voice" section of your ICP research.
- **Proof expectations**: what they need to see to believe (real reviews, relevance, a real face, a guarantee, transparency).
- **Price sensitivity**: where the low-ticket tripwire lands vs where the main offer needs the value-stack + guarantee.
- **Decision-makers**: who actually decides.
- **Likely objections per funnel stage**: ad → the "is this legit?" objection; page → the "will it work for me / will we adopt it?" objection; checkout → the "can I cancel? is the price real?" objection.

## Ready hooks

Draft the page's hooks from the buyer's own phrases above and your message-spine KB — never from
another company's lines. Adapt per segment.

## Reliability rule

Every STAT used on a page carries its reliability flag from your ICP research. Cite primary studies
for hard claims; treat vendor/directional figures as illustrative, not as hard guarantees. Re-verify
before publishing a number as fact.
