# CUT — Message Spine

> The one canonical story. Every surface — App Store listing, landing page, blog, ads,
> emails, social — inherits from this file. If copy contradicts the spine, the copy is wrong
> or the spine needs an owner-approved change. Grounded in `kb/icp-research.md` (2026-07-12)
> and `company.yml`. Claims are capability-framed only — no outcome guarantees, no health or
> medical claims (see `company.yml` → legal.regulated_claims).

## The one-sentence story

**You already know how to cut. CUT makes sure you finish it — one next action at a time.**

## Who it's for (from the ICP beachhead)

The repeat cutter: ~25–40, lifts seriously, has started more than one cut and watched it
fall apart mid-way. Knows the theory (deficit, protein, keep lifting). Fails on execution —
busy weeks, restaurants, stalls, missed logs. Doesn't want another dashboard. Wants to be
told what to do next. [icp-research §Segments]

## The enemy (what we position against)

1. **The tracker** (MyFitnessPal-class): records what happened, decides nothing. "Another
   calorie tracker" fatigue is documented in the audience's own words. [icp §9]
2. **The rigid meal-plan app** (RP-class): the plans work, the rigidity breaks against real
   life — fixed meal times, red adherence scores, guilt at check-in. [icp §8, §12, §19]
3. **The spreadsheet + three apps** stack: all decisions still on you, at the exact moment
   (week 6, tired, stalled) you're least equipped to make them. [icp §5, §13]

We never name competitors in public copy; we describe the experience ("apps that make you
do the math at 9pm").

## The core promise (capability-framed)

CUT looks at your weigh-ins, your training, and where you are in the cut, and gives you
**one clear next action** — so the plan survives contact with real life.

Allowed framings: "tells you what to do next", "one next action", "built for the day you'd
normally fall off", "coach-like direction".
NOT allowed: guaranteed fat-loss amounts or timelines, "lose X lbs", body-composition
outcome promises, anything medical ("safe", "healthy weight loss" as a claim), before/after
implications we can't substantiate.

## The three pillars (every asset leans on at least one)

### 1. Decision, not data
Trackers hand you charts; you still make every call. CUT makes the call: eat this, train
this, weigh in, hold steady. The user's job is to do, not to interpret.
*Evidence: nobody in the competitive set owns the "decide what happens next" job [icp §JTBD].*

### 2. Built for the bad day
Cuts don't fail on knowledge — they fail on adherence: the missed log, the restaurant
dinner, the stalled scale, the shame spiral. CUT's answer to an off-plan day is the next
right action, never a wall of red.
*Evidence: adherence RCT [icp §5]; shame → disengagement research [icp §6]; RP rigidity
complaints [icp §8].*

### 3. Keep the muscle
A cut you finish skinny-fat is a failed cut. Muscle retention is the first-class constraint
in every recommendation — rate of loss, protein, training load — not an afterthought.
*Evidence: fat-free-mass literature [icp §2]; muscle-loss anxiety is mainstream [icp §20].*

## Voice (from company.yml, applied)

Direct, coach-like, zero fluff. Short sentences. A knowledgeable training partner, not a
wellness brand. No hype, no guilt-tripping, no medical claims. Say "you", not "users".
Numbers and specifics beat adjectives. Never shame a miss; never fake urgency.

## Proof we can use today (pre-launch honesty)

We have no users, no testimonials, no outcomes yet. Until we do, proof = specificity:
show the product deciding (screens of a real next action), explain the reasoning the engine
uses in plain words, and cite the public science for the approach (moderate rate, high
protein, keep lifting). **Never invent social proof, user counts, or results.**

## Message hooks (inherit-ready, from the evidence)

- "You don't need more data. You need a decision."
- "Built for week 6, not day 1."
- "The scale stalled. CUT already knows whether to change anything."
- "Ate out? Log it and get the next move. No wall of red."
- "Finish the cut. Keep the muscle."
- "A coach decides for you at $250/month. A tracker decides nothing at $12. CUT decides."
  (price figures must be re-verified before public use — see icp §14 caveat)

## What we never say (banned by evidence + compliance)

- Any outcome guarantee (lbs, %, dates, "you will lose…")
- "Diet" as the noun for the product; "weight loss app"; "calorie tracker"
- Shame/guilt framing ("don't cheat", "stay on track or else", streak-loss threats)
- Medical/health claims ("healthy", "safe", "clinically", "doctor-approved")
- Fake urgency, fake scarcity, invented testimonials or user counts
