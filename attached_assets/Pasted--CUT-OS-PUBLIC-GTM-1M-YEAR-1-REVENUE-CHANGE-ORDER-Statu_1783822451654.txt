# CUT OS — PUBLIC GTM + $1M YEAR-1 REVENUE CHANGE ORDER

## Status

This change order supersedes any assumption that CUT OS is permanently a private, Zarif-only application.

Do **not** discard the native mobile build or restart the project.

Preserve the current React Native + Expo + TypeScript direction, Replit cloud backend, PostgreSQL source-of-truth model, and native QA gates.

Change the architecture and product model now so CUT OS can become a public App Store subscription business.

## Business objective

Build CUT OS with a credible path to:

- public iOS launch
- paid subscription monetization
- approximately **$1,000,000 in gross year-1 revenue**
- healthy consumer software contribution margins
- scalable multi-user architecture
- privacy-conscious growth analytics
- creator / affiliate distribution
- future Android launch

This is a stretch operating target, not a guaranteed forecast.

### Revenue planning assumption

Launch pricing target:

- Monthly: **$19.99**
- Annual: **$99.99**
- Annual should be the primary merchandising offer
- 7-day trial is the initial test default
- no permanent lifetime plan
- no full-featured free tier at initial launch

Planning shorthand:

- target approximately **10,000 first-year paying customers**
- target approximately **$100 first-year gross billed revenue per payer**
- 10,000 × approximately $100 = approximately $1,000,000 gross billed revenue

This is a business planning model, not a hardcoded application calculation.

The application must support pricing experiments and remote paywall configuration rather than embedding prices throughout UI code.

# 1. PRODUCT POSITIONING MUST CHANGE

CUT OS is not positioned as:

- another calorie tracker
- another workout log
- another generic AI fitness coach
- MyFitnessPal with a better interface

Initial market wedge:

> **The adaptive cut operating system for people who lift.**

Core job:

> Tell me exactly what I need to do next to finish my cut without giving up the muscle I already built.

Initial ideal customer profile:

- age approximately 25–40
- has prior lifting experience
- understands basic calories or protein but struggles with consistent execution
- needs to lose approximately 15–30 lb
- works a demanding job, owns a business, or has an inconsistent schedule
- dislikes bland meal plans
- eats restaurant, cultural, family, or mixed cuisine
- has repeatedly started cuts and lost consistency
- wants a system, not more fitness content

Do not gender-lock the underlying product or database.

The first GTM creative and positioning can focus heavily on men who lift because that is the launch wedge.

Founder-first product truth:

CUT OS was originally designed around Zarif's real cut.

Keep a Zarif development/demo seed.

Do not hardcode Zarif's profile, cuisine, measurements, height, training numbers, locations, or goals into public product logic.

# 7. YEAR-1 REVENUE OPERATING MODEL

Do not hardcode this model into the consumer app.

Create `YEAR1_REVENUE_MODEL.md`.

## Base target math

Target:

`$1,000,000 gross billed revenue`

Pricing:

- annual = $99.99
- monthly = $19.99

Planning assumption:

- approximately 75–80% of payers on annual
- monthly payer contributes approximately five paid months inside the measured first-year window on average for planning

At those assumptions, first-year billed revenue per payer is approximately $100.

Therefore:

**approximately 10,000 paying customers are required for approximately $1M gross billed revenue.**

## Acquisition sensitivity

Model at least:

- 2.66% download/first-open to paid
- 4.0%
- 5.5%
- 8.0%
- 10.5%

At approximately 10,000 payers, rough first-open/download requirements are:

- 2.66% = approximately 376,000
- 4.0% = approximately 250,000
- 5.5% = approximately 182,000
- 8.0% = approximately 125,000
- 10.5% = approximately 95,000

Make assumptions editable in documentation or an internal model.

## Margin target

Operating target:

- App Store/platform fees: use actual realized rate
- refunds: monitor actual
- infrastructure + AI variable cost: target below 5% of gross revenue
- creator/affiliate + paid acquisition: manage to a blended contribution model
- target contribution margin after platform fees, refunds, variable infrastructure, and direct acquisition spend:
  **50%+ at scale**

Do not optimize for raw revenue while paid acquisition loses money.

## CAC guardrail

Initial target:

**blended cost per paying customer <= $25**

Stretch:
<= $20

Do not scale a paid channel solely because cost per install is low.

Scale against:
- payer conversion
- realized payer revenue
- refund behavior
- 30-day engagement
- payback

# 15. FINAL PRODUCT PRINCIPLE

The public version of CUT OS must preserve the thing that made Zarif want it:

> He did not want to study another dashboard. He wanted the app to tell him what to do next.

Do not let public-market generalization turn CUT OS into a bloated tracker.

Every new public feature must answer one of:

1. Does this improve the Next Action?
2. Does this make execution easier?
3. Does this make progress interpretation better?
4. Does this improve paid conversion or retention without harming user trust?

If the answer is no, do not put it in the first public release.
